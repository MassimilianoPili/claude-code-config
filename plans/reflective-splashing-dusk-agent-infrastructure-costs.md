## 1. Well Drilling (Pozzo Artesiano) on Granite

### 1.1 Hydrogeology of Sardinian Granite

Sardinian granite (graniti ercinici, Carbonifero-Permiano, 300-280 Ma) is a **crystalline basement rock** with essentially **zero primary porosity**. Water exists only in:
- **Fracture zones** (secondary porosity): joints, faults, weathered zones
- **Regolith** (saprolite): the decomposed granite mantle, typically 5-20m thick in Sardinia
- **Contact zones**: where granite meets metamorphic or sedimentary formations

**Key geological fact:** Gallura granite is massive, coarse-grained (batolite gallurese). Goceano scisti (Paleozoic metamorphics) have slightly better fracture-network connectivity but still qualify as "hard rock aquifers."

[MEDIUM confidence — based on geological surveys of Sardinia, Carmignani et al. 2001]

### 1.2 Typical Depth to Water

| Zone | Substrate | Typical depth | Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------|-------|
| Gallura (Aggius, Tempio) | Massive granite | 40-80 m | 20-120 m | Highly variable — depends on fracture interception |
| Goceano (Bono, Bultei) | Scisti/filladi | 30-60 m | 15-80 m | Better fracture connectivity, shallower on average |
| Weathered zone only | Saprolite | 8-20 m | 5-25 m | Low yield, seasonal — NOT reliable |

**Critical point:** In granite, depth alone does not predict success. A well at 100m in unfractured granite will be dry. A well at 30m that intercepts a major fracture zone can produce 2+ L/s. **Pre-drilling geophysical survey (indagine geoelettrica / sismica a rifrazione) is essential** — cost EUR 800-2,000 but dramatically increases success probability.

[MEDIUM confidence — geological literature + industry practice]

### 1.5 Success Rate

**In crystalline rock (granite/scisti) without pre-drilling survey:**
- ~50-60% chance of finding "some water" (> 0.1 L/s)
- ~30-40% chance of finding "usable water" for irrigation (> 0.5 L/s)
- ~15-20% chance of finding "good water" (> 1 L/s)

**With geophysical survey (geoelettrica + indagine geologica di superficie):**
- ~75-85% chance of finding usable water (> 0.5 L/s)
- ~50-60% chance of finding good water (> 1 L/s)

**This is the single most important recommendation: spend EUR 1,500-2,000 on a geophysical survey BEFORE drilling.** The alternative is gambling EUR 15,000-25,000.

[MEDIUM confidence — based on Italian hydrogeological practice literature; exact percentages vary by source]

### 1.6 Flow Rate Expectations

| Substrate | Typical yield | Good yield | Exceptional |
|-----------|--------------|------------|-------------|
| Gallura granite | 0.2-0.5 L/s | 0.5-1.5 L/s | 2-5 L/s (rare, major fracture) |
| Goceano scisti | 0.3-1.0 L/s | 1.0-2.0 L/s | 3-5 L/s |

For irrigation of 5000 m2 with drip: **0.5 L/s is sufficient** if you have storage (cisterna). You irrigate into the tank, and the drip system draws from the tank.

For reference: 0.5 L/s = 1,800 L/h = 43,200 L/day = 43 m3/day. For 5000 m2 at 5 mm/day water demand = 25 m3/day. So 0.5 L/s running 14h/day covers it.

### 1.8 Permit Process

**Legal framework:**
- R.D. 1775/1933 (Testo Unico Acque) — still in force, heavily amended
- D.Lgs. 152/2006 (Codice dell'Ambiente), Part III
- Piano di Tutela delle Acque (PTA) Sardegna — regional plan

**Authority:** Regione Autonoma della Sardegna, **Servizio Tutela e Gestione delle Risorse Idriche** (was Genio Civile, now reorganized under the Assessorato alla Difesa dell'Ambiente).

**Two types of authorization:**

**A) Pozzo per uso domestico (< 2 L/s, depth < 30m, for personal/household use):**
- Simplified procedure: **denuncia** (notification) to the Comune + Regione
- No concession fee, just administrative costs (~EUR 100-200)
- Must be registered in the catasto dei pozzi
- Timeline: 30-60 days

**B) Pozzo per uso irriguo / produttivo (concessione di derivazione):**
- Full application to the Regione Sardegna
- Requires: relazione geologica (geological report by licensed geologo), progetto del pozzo, planimetria catastale, certificato urbanistico
- Publication on the BURAS (Bollettino Ufficiale) for 30 days for oppositions
- Environmental screening if in protected area or near Natura 2000 sites
- **Timeline: 6-18 months** (bureaucratic reality in Sardinia — budget 12 months)
- **Annual fee (canone):** EUR 0.50-2.00 per 1000 m3 extracted (varies by volume bracket)
- For 25,000 m3/year (5000 m2 irrigated): approximately EUR 12-50/year

**Practical note:** Many agricultural wells in Sardinia operate in a grey area — drilled as "uso domestico" but used for irrigation. This is technically illegal and can result in sanctions (sealing of the well). The legal path is slower but protects the investment.

[HIGH confidence on regulatory framework; MEDIUM on timeline and fees — these vary with administrative efficiency]

## 2. Spring Water Rights (Concessione di Derivazione per Sorgente)

### 2.1 Legal Framework

**Art. 1, R.D. 1775/1933:** "Sono pubbliche tutte le acque sorgenti, fluenti e lacuali, anche se artificialmente estratte dal sottosuolo" — ALL water, including springs, is **public domain**.

**Art. 93, R.D. 1775/1933 (as amended):** Springs on private land — the landowner has **priority** (diritto di preferenza) in obtaining the concession, but still needs one.

**Art. 167, D.Lgs. 152/2006:** Confirms public ownership. Even springs on your own land require authorization.

### 2.2 Exception: Art. 17.2 R.D. 1775/1933

> "E' libera la raccolta di acque piovane in invasi e cisterne al servizio di fondi agricoli"

**This applies ONLY to rainwater (acque piovane), NOT to springs.** A spring (sorgente) is groundwater emerging at the surface — it is legally public water regardless of where it surfaces.

**However:** If the "spring" is actually just seasonal surface runoff collecting in a natural depression on your land, that arguably falls under rainwater collection. The distinction matters and a geologo can document it.

### 2.3 Concession Process for a Spring

**Application to:** Regione Sardegna, Servizio del Genio Civile competente per territorio (Sassari for Gallura, Nuoro for Goceano).

**Required documentation:**
1. Istanza (application form)
2. Relazione tecnica by licensed professional (geologo or ingegnere)
3. Progetto delle opere di captazione (how you'll capture the spring)
4. Planimetria catastale showing spring location
5. Portata della sorgente (measured flow rate — at least 3 seasonal measurements recommended)
6. Destinazione d'uso (irrigation, domestic, livestock)
7. Marca da bollo (EUR 16)

**Process:**
1. Submit to Regione → protocol number assigned
2. Publication on BURAS for 30 days (public notice)
3. Site inspection by regional technician (sopralluogo)
4. Conference of services (conferenza dei servizi) if in sensitive area
5. Decreto di concessione issued

**Timeline:** Officially 90-180 days. Practically **8-18 months** in Sardinia.

**Annual fee:**
- Uso irriguo: EUR 0.50-1.50 per 1000 m3 (same brackets as wells)
- Small concessions (< 5000 m3/year): minimal fee, often EUR 20-50/year total
- Administrative costs at application: EUR 200-500

**Duration:** Concessions are typically granted for **20-30 years**, renewable.

**Maximum extraction:** Set case-by-case based on:
- Measured spring flow (portata minima misurata)
- Minimum vital flow (deflusso minimo vitale) if the spring feeds a watercourse
- PTA constraints for the specific sub-basin
- Typical limit: you can take up to 50-70% of the minimum measured flow

### 2.4 Spring on Your Own Land — Do You Still Need a Concession?

**Yes, legally you do.** Even if the spring is entirely on your property. Italian water law is unambiguous: all groundwater and surface water is public domain (demanio pubblico).

**Practical reality:** For very small springs used for domestic/agricultural purposes on isolated mountain land, enforcement is minimal. But:
- If you invest in infrastructure (captazione, condotte, vasca), you want legal protection
- A neighbor or municipality could claim the water if you lack a concession
- The concession gives you a **legal right** (titolo concessorio) that is defensible in court

**Recommendation:** Apply for the concession. The cost is minimal (EUR 500-1,000 including the geologo's relazione), the timeline is long but the concession protects a 20-30 year investment.

[HIGH confidence on legal framework — R.D. 1775/1933 and D.Lgs. 152/2006 are well-established. MEDIUM confidence on Sardinian administrative timelines.]

## 3. Rainwater Harvesting

### 3.1 Collection Potential

**Formula:** Annual collection = Roof area x Annual rainfall x Runoff coefficient

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Roof area | 200 m2 |
| Annual rainfall at 600-800m Sardinia | 800-1000 mm/year (typical for Goceano/Gallura highlands) |
| Runoff coefficient (tile/metal roof) | 0.80-0.90 |
| Runoff coefficient (green/rough surface) | 0.50-0.70 |

**Calculation for tile/metal roof:**
- Low estimate: 200 m2 x 0.800 m x 0.80 = **128 m3/year** (128,000 liters)
- High estimate: 200 m2 x 1.000 m x 0.90 = **180 m3/year** (180,000 liters)

**Seasonal distribution matters enormously:**
- October-March: ~70% of annual rainfall (high collection period)
- April-September: ~30% (low collection, HIGH irrigation demand)
- You collect most water when you need it least

**Implication:** Need storage to bridge the dry season. For 5000 m2 irrigated at even 2 mm/day for 120 summer days = 1,200 m3. Rainwater alone (128-180 m3/year) covers only **10-15% of irrigation need**. It's a supplement, not the primary source.

[HIGH confidence on rainfall data and calculations]

### 3.3 Regulation

**Art. 17.2 R.D. 1775/1933:** "E' libera la raccolta di acque piovane in invasi e cisterne al servizio di fondi agricoli."

**Translation:** Rainwater collection in tanks and small reservoirs serving agricultural land is **FREE and unrestricted** — no concession, no permit for the water itself.

**Building permits for the cisterna:**
- Cisterna interrata < 50 m3 on agricultural land: typically **SCIA** (Segnalazione Certificata di Inizio Attivita) or even **Edilizia Libera** depending on the municipality
- Cisterna interrata > 50 m3: may require Permesso di Costruire depending on local PRG
- Check with the Comune's Ufficio Tecnico — rules vary

**Sardinian regional specifics:**
- No additional regional restrictions on rainwater harvesting
- The Piano di Tutela delle Acque actually **encourages** rainwater harvesting for agriculture
- Some PSR Sardegna (Piano di Sviluppo Rurale) funds have historically included cisterne among eligible investments — check current programming period 2023-2027

### 3.4 Can You Fill a Garden Pond with Rainwater Only?

**Yes, unambiguously legal.** A pond (laghetto) filled exclusively by:
- Direct rainfall on its surface
- Runoff from roofs via gutters/pipes
- Surface runoff from your own land

...requires **no water concession**. It falls under Art. 17.2 R.D. 1775/1933.

**However:** If the pond intercepts a watercourse (even seasonal) or a spring, it becomes a "derivazione" and requires a concession. The distinction is important during site design.

**Practical advice:** Design the pond so it is clearly disconnected from any natural watercourse. Document this with a geological/topographic survey. If inspectors visit, you want clear evidence that the water source is rain only.

[HIGH confidence on the legal framework]

**Stocking density:** For a 100 m2 x 1.2m pond = 120 m3 = 120,000 L. Rule of thumb for koi: 1 adult koi per 5,000-10,000 L. So **12-24 adult koi maximum.** Start with 10-15 juveniles and let them grow.

[MEDIUM confidence on koi prices — market fluctuates, and post-COVID the hobby market shifted]

## 6. Electricity Connection on Rural Land

### 6.1 Grid Connection (Allacciamento E-Distribuzione)

**E-Distribuzione (ex ENEL Distribuzione)** manages the low-voltage grid in Sardinia.

**Cost structure for new connection (nuova connessione) on agricultural land:**

The cost depends on:
1. **Distance from nearest transformer (cabina MT/BT)**
2. **Power requested (kW)**
3. **Complexity of the connection**

**ARERA-regulated costs (Delibera 568/2019 and updates):**

| Component | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| **Fixed contribution** (quota fissa) | ~EUR 85 |
| **Power-based contribution** (quota potenza) | EUR 70-80 per kW requested |
| **Distance-based contribution** (quota distanza) | EUR 100-200 per meter beyond 200m from transformer |
| **Administrative costs** | ~EUR 30 |

**Typical scenarios:**

| Scenario | Distance | Power | Estimated cost |
|----------|----------|-------|----------------|
| Near transformer (< 200m) | 100m | 3 kW | 500-800 EUR |
| Medium distance | 500m | 3 kW | 2,000-5,000 EUR |
| Remote agricultural | 1,000m | 3 kW | 5,000-15,000 EUR |
| Very remote mountain | 2,000m+ | 3 kW | 15,000-40,000+ EUR |

**Key facts:**
- The first 200m from the transformer are "free" (included in fixed costs)
- Beyond 200m, you pay per meter — and in mountain terrain this includes poles (pali) on difficult terrain
- If a new transformer is needed: EUR 10,000-25,000+ (you typically share this cost with E-Distribuzione)
- Timeline: 30-120 working days after acceptance (in practice: 3-12 months in Sardinia)

**Typical distance from nearest transformer in mountain Sardinia:**
- Near a village (Bono, Aggius): 200-1,000m
- Isolated agricultural land: 1,000-3,000m
- Very remote: 3,000m+ (grid connection may be impractical)

**For agricultural land 1km from nearest transformer:** Budget **EUR 5,000-15,000** for the connection itself.

[HIGH confidence on ARERA-regulated cost structure; MEDIUM on actual distances which are site-specific]

### 6.3 Cost Comparison: Grid vs Solar

| Factor | Grid connection | Off-grid solar (1.6 kWp) |
|--------|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Upfront cost** (1km distance) | 5,000-15,000 EUR | 4,300-8,200 EUR |
| **Annual electricity cost** | 300-600 EUR/year (3kW contract, light use) | ~0 EUR (maintenance only) |

| **Scalability** | Unlimited (up to contracted power) | Limited by panel/battery size |

**Recommendation:**
- **If < 500m from transformer:** Grid connection is clearly better (EUR 500-2,000 + reliable power)

- **If > 1500m from transformer:** Off-grid solar is almost certainly cheaper and faster
- **Hybrid approach:** Grid connection for reliability + small solar for pump/irrigation = best of both worlds if grid is affordable

**Tax incentives:** The Superbonus/Bonus Casa framework has changed repeatedly. As of early 2025, battery storage and solar panels on agricultural buildings may qualify for **50% deduction over 10 years** (detrazione IRPEF). Check current status with a commercialista.

## 7. Internet Connectivity in Rural Mountain Sardinia

### 7.1 4G Coverage

**Bono (Goceano, 540m):**
- TIM: Good 4G coverage in town, degrades 1-2 km outside
- Vodafone: Moderate 4G coverage
- WindTre: Moderate 4G
- Iliad: Limited (relies on WindTre RAN sharing in rural Sardinia)
- **Typical speed in town:** 20-60 Mbps down, 5-15 Mbps up
- **1-2 km outside town:** 5-20 Mbps down, variable

**Aggius (Gallura, 514m):**
- TIM: 4G present, moderate coverage
- Vodafone: 4G present
- WindTre: Limited
- **Typical speed:** 15-40 Mbps down

**Santu Lussurgiu (Montiferru, 500m):**
- TIM: 4G coverage in town
- Vodafone: 4G, moderate
- **More challenging outside town** due to terrain shadowing

**For agricultural land OUTSIDE town:** Coverage drops significantly. Mountains create shadow zones. A directional external 4G antenna (antenna esterna direzionale) can dramatically improve signal:
- **4G router with external antenna:** Huawei B818 + MIMO external antenna = EUR 200-400
- **Performance gain:** 2-5x speed improvement, from "barely usable" to "workable"

**Recommendation:** Before committing to a mobile plan, test coverage at the EXACT location with a SIM from each major operator. Use the OpenSignal app or similar to measure actual speeds.

[MEDIUM confidence — coverage maps change and are notoriously unreliable for rural areas]

### 7.2 Fixed Wireless (FWA)

**Eolo (now part of Open Fiber ecosystem):**
- **Coverage:** Has base stations in many Sardinian mountain towns including Bono area
- **Technology:** Fixed wireless 5G/LTE
- **Speed:** 30-100 Mbps down (theoretical), 15-30 Mbps typical in rural mountain
- **Cost:** EUR 25-35/month
- **Equipment:** Outdoor CPE antenna, free installation (with contract)
- **Limitation:** Line of sight to base station required — mountain terrain can block
- **Check:** eolo.it coverage checker with exact address

**Linkem/ex-Tiscali FWA:**
- Similar technology, less coverage in interior Sardinia
- Being absorbed into other operators

**Open Fiber FWA:**
- Part of the BUL (Banda Ultra Larga) public subsidy plan
- Rolling out fixed wireless in areas where fiber is not economically viable
- Check openfiber.it for planned coverage

[MEDIUM confidence — FWA availability changes rapidly with new base stations]

### 7.3 Starlink

**Legal status in Italy:** Fully legal. SpaceX's Italian subsidiary (Starlink Italia S.r.l.) is registered with AGCOM and authorized to operate.

**Current pricing (Italy, 2025):**

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Hardware (antenna + router) | EUR 349-449 (one-time, was 499, has been reduced) |
| Monthly subscription (Residential) | EUR 40-50/month |
| Monthly subscription (Roaming/RV) | EUR 55-75/month |

**Performance:**
- Download: 50-200 Mbps (typical 80-120 Mbps)
- Upload: 10-30 Mbps
- Latency: 25-50 ms (much improved from early days)
- Reliability: 99%+ uptime

**Advantages for mountain Sardinia:**
- Works ANYWHERE with clear sky view (no trees blocking to the north for low-orbit passes)
- No dependence on local infrastructure
- Good enough for video calls, remote work, smart home, etc.
- Self-install in 15 minutes

**Disadvantages:**
- Monthly cost higher than terrestrial broadband
- Needs power (50-75W average consumption)
- Slight latency spikes during peak hours
- Antenna can struggle in heavy rain/snow (rare in Sardinia)
- Needs clear sky view — if surrounded by trees, may need a roof mount or elevated pole

**Verdict:** For rural mountain Sardinia, **Starlink is currently the most reliable option** for proper internet. It eliminates the dependency on local infrastructure.

[HIGH confidence — Starlink Italy operations are well-documented]

### 7.4 Fiber (BUL / Piano Italia 1 Giga)

**Piano BUL (Banda Ultra Larga):**
- Open Fiber won the public subsidy contracts for "aree bianche" (market-failure areas) in Sardinia
- FTTH (Fiber To The Home) deployments are ongoing but extremely behind schedule
- **Bono:** Listed in BUL plans, but actual deployment status varies — check bandaultralarga.italia.it
- **Aggius:** Some fiber deployment in progress (Gallura has received more attention)
- **Santu Lussurgiu:** Listed but likely delayed

**Piano Italia 1 Giga (PNRR funded):**
- EUR 3.8 billion national plan to bring 1 Gbps to underserved areas
- Sardinia is a priority region
- Target completion: 2026 (original), realistically 2027-2028 for mountain areas
- Includes both FTTH and FWA in the most remote areas

**Practical reality:** Do NOT count on fiber arriving at isolated agricultural land in the next 2-3 years. Even "in-town" fiber deployments in mountain Sardinia are running 1-2 years behind schedule.

**Recommendation order for rural mountain Sardinia:**
1. **Starlink** — available now, reliable, moderate cost
2. **Eolo FWA** — if line-of-sight to a base station exists, cheaper monthly
3. **4G with external antenna** — backup/secondary, depends on coverage
4. **Fiber** — may arrive eventually, don't plan around it

[MEDIUM confidence — BUL deployment schedules are notoriously unreliable]

## Key Recommendations

1. **Geophysical survey FIRST** (EUR 1,500-2,000) — before committing to a well location. This is the highest-ROI expense in the entire project.

2. **If a spring exists on the land:** Apply for the concession immediately. The timeline is 8-18 months, so start NOW even before other construction.

3. **Cisterna is essential regardless of water source.** Even with a well, you need storage to buffer between well output and irrigation demand. 30 m3 minimum, 50 m3 preferred.

4. **Granite excavation is the cost wildcard.** For the pond, invest in a geological survey to find the deepest regolith zone. Consider a berm/above-ground design to reduce granite excavation.

5. **Grid vs solar:** Get the E-Distribuzione quote (free — submit preventivo online) before deciding. If > EUR 10,000 for grid connection, solar is clearly better for irrigation-only use.

6. **Internet:** Get Starlink now, re-evaluate when Eolo or fiber arrives.

7. **Permits timeline:** Start the concessione di derivazione and any building permits (SCIA for cisterna) immediately. These are the longest lead-time items.
