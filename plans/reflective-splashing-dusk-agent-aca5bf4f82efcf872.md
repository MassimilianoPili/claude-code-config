### 6. San Leonardo de Siete Fuentes

**What it is**: A tiny hamlet (frazione) of Santu Lussurgiu, at about 700m altitude on the slopes of Montiferru. Named for its seven natural springs ("siete fuentes") and a Romanesque church of San Leonardo dei Cavalieri di Malta (12th century, Knights of the Order of St. John).

**Access**: Freely accessible. It is a public hamlet, not a fenced site. You drive or walk in.
- **Distance from Santu Lussurgiu**: ~5 km north, road SP15.
- **Parking**: Small parking area at the entrance of the hamlet. Free.
- **Church**: Usually open during daytime (check locally; in summer it may have regular hours, in off-season it may be closed but the exterior and springs are always accessible).
- **The springs**: The seven fountains are scattered around the hamlet and are freely accessible year-round. The water is drinkable.
- **No entrance fee**.
- **Best time**: Spring and autumn. In summer it is a popular local picnic/escape-the-heat destination.

### 7. Villa Piercy / Badde Salighes (Bolotana)

**What it is** (from Wikipedia IT + SardegnaTurismo):
- English-style villa built 1879-1882 by **Benjamin Piercy**, the Welsh engineer who built Sardinia's railway network.
- Located in **Badde Salighes** ("valley of the willows"), municipality of Bolotana, province of Nuoro.
- Eclectic style, 2 floors + semi-basement, quadrangular plan with 4 corner turrets with metal cupolas.
- Coordinates: 40.350278 N, 8.88 E

**The garden** (4 hectares, English-style):
- Mix of native species (holm oak, downy oak, chestnut, holly, yew, Montpellier maple) and exotic species imported by Piercy (Himalayan cedar, incense cedar, evergreen boxwood, Spanish fir).
- Famous **boxwood tunnel** — one of the longest in Italy.
- Swimming pool surrounded by trees.
- The property originally extended over 3,700 hectares.

**Visiting**:
- The villa and garden are **visitable** — they are managed by the municipality of Bolotana (the property was eventually sold/donated to public hands).
- **Hours**: [VERIFY with Comune di Bolotana, tel. 0785 420025]. Typically open in spring-summer with guided visits. In autumn, check if open — may require appointment.
- **Entrance fee**: Small fee (likely 3-5 EUR) or free [VERIFY].
- **Contact**: Comune di Bolotana or Pro Loco Bolotana. The SardegnaTurismo page describes it as: "A Bolotana, al centro della Sardegna, un giardino di piante autoctone affiancate da varieta esotiche e singolari incroci arborei fa da cornice a una bella villa inglese."

**Driving from Santu Lussurgiu to Villa Piercy**:
- Route: Santu Lussurgiu --> SS129bis --> Macomer --> SS129 east --> Bolotana --> follow signs to Badde Salighes
- Distance: ~45 km, time: ~50-60 min (mountain roads)

### 8. Cascate di Sos Molinos

**What it is**: A waterfall on the Rio Mannu, on the slopes of Montiferru, in the territory of Santu Lussurgiu.

**Access**:
- Reachable on foot from the area of San Leonardo de Siete Fuentes or from the road between Santu Lussurgiu and Cuglieri.
- There is a marked hiking trail (sentiero) — typically starting near the provincial road. The walk is approximately 30-45 minutes one way (moderate difficulty, some uneven terrain).
- **Best time**: After autumn/winter rains. In summer the waterfall may be reduced to a trickle.
- **Parking**: At the trailhead (informal dirt parking area). [VERIFY exact location — ask at the Pro Loco of Santu Lussurgiu]
- **No entrance fee**. Open access.
- **Footwear**: Hiking boots recommended, the path can be slippery.

### 6. Sos Nibberos (Foresta dei Tassi)

**What it is**: A relic forest of **yew trees (Taxus baccata)** on the slopes of **Monte Rasu** (1,259m), in the territory of Bono. One of the most important yew forests in the Mediterranean. Some trees are estimated to be over 500 years old. "Nibberos" means "yews" in Sardinian.

**Access**:
- **Freely accessible**: It is a public forest managed by the Agenzia Forestas (Sardinian forestry agency). No permit needed.
- **Guided tours**: The Agenzia Forestas or the Comune di Bono may organize guided tours, especially in spring/summer. Contact Agenzia Forestas (Servizio Territoriale di Nuoro) or Pro Loco Bono [VERIFY phone numbers].
- **Parking**: There is a small parking/clearing area at the entrance to the forest. The last stretch of road may be unpaved.
- **Trail**: A marked path (sentiero) leads through the forest. Easy to moderate difficulty, approximately 2-3 km loop.
- **Best time**: Autumn (for colors and mushroom season) or spring.
- **No entrance fee**.

### 7. Monte Pisanu Chestnut Area

**What it is**: The chestnut forests on the lower slopes of Monte Rasu / Monte Pisanu, between Bono and the Goceano hills. Ancient chestnut groves (castagneti) that produce excellent chestnuts.

- **Marked trail**: There are forestry tracks and some marked paths. Ask locally at the Comune di Bono or look for CAI-marked trails. [VERIFY specific trail names]
- **Parking**: Along the provincial road towards Monte Rasu, there are pullover areas near the chestnut groves.
- **Best time**: **Late October to mid-November** for chestnut harvesting and autumn foliage.

### 8. Valle della Luna, Aggius

**What it is**: A landscape of dramatic **granite formations** eroded into surreal shapes, in the territory of Aggius (Gallura). Named "Valley of the Moon" for its lunar appearance. Wikipedia redirects Valle della Luna (Aggius) to the Aggius page, section "Luoghi di interesse naturalistico."

**Access**:
- **Location**: South of Aggius, along the road towards Tempio Pausania (SP74).
- **Parking**: There is a designated parking area along the SP74, with signs for "Valle della Luna." Free.
- **Entrance fee**: **Free**. It is an open natural landscape.
- **Trail**: A marked path leads through the granite formations. Easy to moderate, approximately 1-2 km loop. Some scrambling over rocks possible for closer exploration.
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours for a good walk.
- **Aggius itself** is worth visiting: the MEOC (Museo Etnografico Olien) museum, the traditional granite architecture.

### 9. Curadoreddu Picnic Area & Mouflons Reserve

**What it is**: Curadoreddu (also spelled Curadureddu) is a picnic/rest area on the SP38 road that climbs Monte Limbara from Tempio Pausania. It is within the forested area of Limbara.

- **Parking**: Yes, there is a parking area with picnic tables.
- **Mouflons reserve**: There is an enclosure/reserve for **mouflons (Ovis aries musimon)** in the Limbara area, managed by the forestry service. ## GENERAL QUESTIONS

### Best Season to Visit

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Mid-October to early November** | **BEST FOR AUTUMN COLORS.** Chestnuts falling, oaks turning, yews in contrast. Mild temperatures (10-18C at altitude). Mushroom season. | Days are shorter. Some attractions may have reduced hours. Rain possible (but usually brief). |
| **April-May** | Landscape is **green and lush**, wildflowers everywhere. Pleasant temperatures. Longer days. | No autumn colors. Some mountain roads may still be muddy from winter. |
| **June** | Everything is green, warm but not hot at altitude. Longest days. | Starts getting dry in lowlands. Tourist season beginning (but mountains stay quiet). |
| **September** | Still warm, landscape turning golden. Few tourists. | Vegetation is dry/brown after summer. Not yet autumn colors. |

**Recommendation**: For autumn colors + chestnuts + yew forest atmosphere: **late October** (around 20-31 October). For green landscape: **late April to mid-May**.

### Kit pH Suolo: Where to Buy in Sardinia

- **Leroy Merlin Sassari**: Via Predda Niedda Nord Strada 34. Has garden section with soil test kits. Distance from Alghero: ~30 km (25 min). Distance from Olbia: ~115 km.
- **Leroy Merlin Cagliari**: If flying via Cagliari. Via Santa Gilla.
- **Brico Io / Bricocenter**: May have basic pH test strips. Check locations in Sassari and Olbia.
- **Agrarie (agricultural supply stores)**: In Sassari there are several agrarie (e.g., Ferrara Severino, Puggioni) that sell professional soil testing kits. In Macomer/Nuoro too (closer to Santu Lussurgiu).
- **Pharmacies**: For basic pH paper strips (cartina tornasole / cartina indicatore universale pH), any pharmacy will have them. These work fine for a rough soil pH test.
- **Amazon.it**: Order before departure. A soil pH meter or test kit (e.g., Neudorff pH Bodentest, ~8-12 EUR) shipped to your home before the trip is the most reliable option.

**Recommendation**: Buy a proper soil pH test kit online before departure. If you forget, stop at Leroy Merlin Sassari on the way from Alghero airport, or at an agraria in Macomer.
