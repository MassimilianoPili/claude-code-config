# Deep Analysis: Three Sardinian Zones for Japanese Garden Suitability

## Research Summary

**Epistemic status:** Mixed -- geological and botanical data are well-sourced (T7 Wikipedia backed by references to geological surveys, T1 Beccaluva et al. 1977 for Montiferru petrology, SISS 1996 for Goceano soils, Piano Gestione Distretto Idrografico for precipitation). Climate data has moderate confidence (official pluviometric averages from regional hydrological plan). Soil pH values are inferred from parent rock geology (high confidence for direction, moderate for exact values). Temperature data is the weakest point -- no station-specific averages were located for these small municipalities; estimates derive from altitude corrections and nearby stations (Macomer, Fonni).

**Confidence:** Medium-High for geology/water; Medium for climate; Low-Medium for exact soil pH measurements.

## ZONE 1: GOCEANO (North-Central Sardinia)

### 1.1 Geology

**Substrate: Paleozoic schists + granitic intrusions** (T7 -- Wikipedia/Bono, confirmed by sardegnaambiente.it environmental assessment referencing "substrati silicei (graniti e metamorfiti)" for "catena del Marghine-Goceano")

The Catena del Goceano is part of the Variscan (Hercynian) basement of Sardinia, formed ~300 Ma. Monte Rasu (1259m) is composed of:
- **Paleozoic metamorphic rocks**: predominantly schists (micaschists, phyllites)
- **Granitic intrusions**: Hercynian granitoids intruding the schist basement
- The castle of Burgos sits on "una rupe granitica isolata" (an isolated granitic cliff) -- direct confirmation of granite presence

**Soil pH implication**: Both schist and granite weather to **strongly acidic soils (pH 4.5-5.5)**. This is the ideal substrate for Japanese gardens. Granitic soils in Sardinia (e.g., Gallura) are well-documented as acidic. The SISS 1996 bulletin specifically studied soils in the Goceano-Monte Pisano forest complex -- the reference mentions the area alongside acidic substrates.

**Confidence: HIGH** -- multiple independent sources confirm siliceous/granitic basement.

### 1.2 Forests of Goceano

**Complesso Forestale del Goceano** -- managed by Agenzia Forestas, consists of several forests:

**Foresta di Burgos / Monte Pisanu** (1,806 ha):
- **Dominant species**: Holm oak (*Quercus ilex*), Downy oak (*Quercus pubescens*), Minor maple (*Acer monspessulanum*)
- At ex-forestry barracks (861m): planted Cedrus atlantica, Thuja gigantea, Abies alba
- Source: Wikipedia/Bono, SardegnaTurismo

**Foresta di Fiorentini** (Bultei territory):
- "Polmone verde" rising to 1000m in the Goceano mountains
- Part of the same complex, connected to Forest'Anela
- Source: SardegnaTurismo official

**Forest'Anela** (1,046 ha):
- "Impiantata nel 1886, e formata da lecci, roverelle e..." (planted 1886, holm oaks and downy oaks)
- Near Anela, Bultei, and Bono
- Source: SardegnaTurismo/Anela

**Sos Nibberos** (Monte Rasu NW slopes, ~1000m):
- **Italy's largest Taxus baccata (yew) forest** -- declared natural monument 1994
- Millennial specimens reaching 16m height, >1m diameter
- Understory: holly (*Ilex aquifolium*), ferns, endemic *Rubus arrigonii*
- Source: Wikipedia/Bono

**Soil type**: On granitic-schist substrate = **acidic soils confirmed by vegetation indicators** (yew, holly, heather communities thrive on acid substrates)

### 1.3 Chestnuts -- CONFIRMED

**"Castagneto a Monte Pisanu - Bono - Bottidda"** -- SardegnaTurismo official page uses this as the gallery image title for Monte Pisanu. Chestnut groves (*Castanea sativa*) are present on the slopes of Monte Pisanu between Bono and Bottidda.

**Significance**: Chestnuts are a powerful acidophilic indicator. *Castanea sativa* requires pH 4.5-6.0 and will NOT grow on calcareous/alkaline soils. Their confirmed presence in Goceano is strong evidence of acidic soils suitable for Acer palmatum (Japanese maple), Rhododendron, Azalea, Camellia, and other Japanese garden staples.

**Confidence: HIGH** -- official tourism source with photo documentation.

### 1.4 Rainfall

**No direct station data found for Bono/Burgos specifically.** However:

The Piano di Gestione del Distretto Idrografico della Sardegna does NOT list Bono/Burgos in the precipitation table I accessed (the table focused on Oristano province). However, by analogy:
- The Goceano is on the LEEWARD side of the prevailing Mistral (NW wind), shielded by the catena itself
- Nearby reference: Barbagia stations at similar altitude (Fonni 1000m = ~1000mm/yr, Desulo ~900mm)
- Estimated Goceano precipitation: **750-900 mm/year** at village altitude (500-580m), potentially **1000+ mm** at Monte Rasu summit (1259m)
- The Wikipedia article on Goceano states the name may derive from "guttiu" (drop/drip), indicating "ricchezza di sorgenti d'acqua" (abundance of water springs)

**Confidence: LOW-MEDIUM** -- estimate by altitude/position analogy, not direct measurement.

### 1.5 Water Resources

**Excellent water availability:**
- The Tirso (Sardinia's longest river, 152km) flows through the southern part of Goceano territory
- Monte Rasu slopes: "fitta rete di limpide sorgenti, ruscelli e torrenti" (dense network of clear springs, streams and torrents) feeding the Tirso
- "Numerosi laghetti" (numerous small lakes) in the mountain zone
- **Diga del Tirso / Lago Omodeo**: the large reservoir is downstream (near Oristano), not immediately adjacent, but the Tirso headwaters flow through Goceano

**Confidence: HIGH** -- multiple sources confirm abundant water.

### 1.6 Accessibility

- **Sassari**: ~60 km, approximately 1 hour by SS131/SS128bis
- **Nuoro**: ~40 km, approximately 45 min
- **Nearest hospital**: Ospedale Civile di Nuoro (Ospedale San Francesco), ~45 min. Also Sassari hospital ~1h.
- **Airport**: Alghero-Fertilia ~1h30, Olbia ~1h45
- Road quality: decent provincial roads, but winding mountain roads for the last stretch

**Confidence: MEDIUM** -- distances verified, travel times estimated.

### 1.7 Wind Exposure

**Moderately exposed.** The Catena del Goceano runs roughly NW-SE. Villages on the eastern/southern slopes (Bono, Burgos) receive some shelter from the Mistral (NW). However, the ridge itself and higher elevations are wind-exposed.

- Monte Rasu summit: very exposed to Mistral
- Villages at 500-580m: partially sheltered by the mountain mass
- Valley floor (Tirso): sheltered

A garden site should be chosen on the **eastern or southern slope**, preferably in a valley fold, NOT on the ridge or plateau.

**Confidence: MEDIUM** -- inferred from topography and wind direction.

## ZONE 2: MONTIFERRU (West-Central Sardinia)

### 2.1 Geology -- CRITICAL ANALYSIS

**Montiferru is an extinct volcanic complex**, active 3.9-1.6 Ma (Pliocene-Pleistocene).

**Rock types present** (T7 Wikipedia IT, confirmed by T1 Beccaluva et al. 1977, Bulletin Volcanologique):

| Rock type | Location/form | pH tendency |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Basalt/basanite/hawaiite** | Altopiano di Abbasanta (east), coastal cliffs, dicchi (dikes) | Neutral to slightly acid (pH 6.0-7.0) |
| **Trachyte** | Dome formations, summit area | Slightly acid (pH 5.5-6.5) |
| **Phonolite** | Grey domes ("grigi duomi fonolitici"), including Rocca Freari (690m) | Slightly acid (pH 5.5-6.5) |
| **Ignimbrite (rhyolitic/rhyodacitic)** | Older Oligocene-Miocene phase | Acidic (pH 4.5-5.5) |
| **Andesite** | Mixed with ignimbrites | Slightly acid (pH 5.5-6.5) |
| **Tephritic/basanitic** | Late phase eruptions | Neutral (pH 6.5-7.0) |

**CRITICAL QUESTION: Is Montiferru soil acidic enough for Japanese maples?**

**Answer: It depends on the EXACT location.**

- The **summit area** (Santu Lussurgiu sits in the crater/conca at 500m on the eastern slope) is dominated by **trachyte and phonolite**. These weather to moderately acidic soils, typically **pH 5.5-6.5**.
- The **eastern plateau** (Abbasanta) is basaltic = **pH 6.0-7.0** -- borderline to too alkaline.
- The **older ignimbritic deposits** are the most acidic = **pH 4.5-5.5** but are localized.

**For Japanese maples (need pH < 6.5):** The trachytic/phonolitic soils around Santu Lussurgiu itself are likely **marginally suitable (pH ~6.0-6.5)**, but NOT as ideal as granite (Goceano). The soils can be amended with sulfur/peat, but the starting point is less favorable than Goceano.

**Additional evidence: Chestnut and holly presence.** Wikipedia Montiferru confirms "impiantati dall'uomo sono anche il castagno" (chestnuts planted by man) and natural holly forests ("fitte formazioni di agrifogli" at Sos Olostriches). Holly (*Ilex aquifolium*) tolerates pH 4.5-6.5 and indicates acidic conditions. The successful growth of chestnuts confirms soils are at least below pH 6.5 in the upper Montiferru.

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH** for rock types (peer-reviewed geology). MEDIUM for soil pH (inferred from parent rock, not direct measurement). The chestnut/holly indicator data raises confidence that upper Montiferru soils ARE acidic enough.

### 2.2 Rainfall -- VERIFIED: EXCELLENT

**Official data from Piano di Gestione del Distretto Idrografico della Sardegna:**

| Location | Mean annual precipitation |
|----------|--------------------------|
| **Santu Lussurgiu** | **960 mm** |
| Bonarcado | 900 mm |
| Scano di Montiferro | 820 mm |
| Monte Urtigu summit (1050m) | **>1100 mm** |
| Cuglieri (450m, western slope) | >800 mm |
| Tresnuraghes (coast) | ~700 mm |

**Source: Piano Gestione Distretto Idrografico, Allegato 1, Tabella "Precipitazioni medie annuali dei comuni" -- government hydrological data.**

**Mechanism**: Orographic rainfall. Wikipedia Montiferru explains: "L'area viene colpita dai venti umidi provenienti dal mare, in particolare dal maestrale, che giunge frontalmente dalla valle del Rodano, nella Francia meridionale, caricandosi di umidita nel suo tragitto sopra il mare." The Mistral hits the Montiferru massif frontally, causing intense orographic precipitation. Additionally, significant "precipitazioni occulte" (occult precipitation = fog drip condensation) increases effective water availability.

This is **among the highest rainfall in Sardinia** outside the Gennargentu. For comparison: Cagliari ~430mm, Sassari ~540mm, Nuoro ~680mm.

**Confidence: HIGH** -- official government hydrological data.

### 2.3 Temperature

No direct station data for Santu Lussurgiu. The nearest station is **Macomer** (17 km NE, same altitude ~500m but more exposed).

**Estimates for Santu Lussurgiu at 500m:**
- **July average**: ~23-25 C (cooler than lowlands due to altitude + wind + cloud cover)
- **January average**: ~5-7 C
- **Frost frequency**: Wikipedia says Macomer is "piu esposto ai venti e alle gelate rispetto a Santu Lussurgiu" (more exposed to winds and frost than S.L.)
- **Climatic classification**: Zona D, 1684 GG (heating degree-days) -- this indicates a moderately cold winter climate for Italy

Santu Lussurgiu's position **inside the volcanic bowl** provides thermal buffering. The town is described as "racchiusa fra i monti" (enclosed between mountains), with Mistral arriving "piu attenuato" (more attenuated) compared to other Montiferru locations.

**Confidence: LOW-MEDIUM** -- no direct temperature records found; estimates from classification and comparative positioning.

### 2.4 Water Resources -- EXCEPTIONAL

Santu Lussurgiu is **extraordinarily water-rich**:

- **San Leonardo de Siete Fuentes**: famous springs ("seven fountains"), water is commercially bottled
- **Elighes Uttiosos** (~1000m): "lecci gocciolanti" (dripping holm oaks) -- springs emerging from roots of ancient oaks
- **Sa Preda Lada**, **Silvanis**, **Sos Crabalzos**: multiple named springs at various altitudes
- **Fontana di Su Sauccu**: spring inside the town itself
- **Sorgenti di Sant'Antioco** (near Scano): among the LARGEST springs in Sardinia, **200 liters/second** in winter/spring
- Multiple rivers: Riu Mannu, Riu di Mare Foghe (48 km)

The volcanic rock permeability creates large underground aquifers: "rocce vulcaniche che sono tanto piu permeabili a seconda della presenza o meno di fessurazioni... andando ad alimentare i solitamente piuttosto grandi bacini sotterranei."

**Etymology**: The name "Lussurgiu" derives from San Lussorio (a martyr saint), NOT from "luxor/water" as the query hypothesized.

**Confidence: HIGH** -- comprehensive Wikipedia article with named springs and flow rates.

### 2.5 Cherry Trees at Bonarcado

Bonarcado is indeed known for cherry production. Cherries (*Prunus avium*) prefer:
- Well-drained soils
- pH 6.0-7.0 (slightly acid to neutral)
- Cool winters for chilling hours (600-1200 hours below 7 C)
- Moderate summer heat

**What this tells us**: The Bonarcado-Montiferru area has adequate winter chill (confirming cool winters), well-drained volcanic soils, and a pH compatible with cherries (6.0-7.0). This is **consistent with but slightly less acidic than** ideal Japanese maple conditions. Cherries are more pH-tolerant than acid-loving plants.

### 2.6 2021 Wildfire Warning

**Critical risk factor**: On July 23-31, 2021, catastrophic wildfires destroyed **>20,000 hectares** in the Montiferru area, affecting Bonarcado, Santu Lussurgiu, Scano, Sennariolo, and Cuglieri. Damages exceeded 1 billion euros. The fire destroyed forests including chestnut and holm oak. The Montiferru is still recovering.

This is relevant for: (a) potential site selection (avoid recently burned areas), (b) long-term fire risk assessment, (c) availability of mature tree cover for garden microclimate.

## ZONE 3: SARCIDANO / LACONI (South-Central Sardinia)

### 3.1 Parco Aymerich, Laconi -- THE KEY PRECEDENT

**Size**: 22 hectares (commonly cited figure). The park surrounds the ruins of Castello Aymerich and Palazzo Aymerich.

**Exotic trees documented** (T7 ISPRA publication "Frutti dimenticati e biodiversita recuperata"):
- **Cedrus libani** (Cedar of Lebanon) -- explicitly mentioned: "poderosi... Cedrus libani del Parco Aymerich a Laconi"
- **Cedrus atlantica** (Atlas cedar)
- Eucalyptus (described as "poderosi eucalitti")
- Various Mediterranean and exotic species

**Watering**: The Italian Wikipedia states Laconi territory "possiede decine di sorgenti" (possesses dozens of springs). The park contains waterfalls -- these are fed by natural springs, not artificial irrigation. The springs emerge from the geological contact zone between different rock formations.

**Current condition**: The park has Bandiera Arancione certification from Touring Club Italiano (quality tourism/environment marker), suggesting it is maintained in good condition.

**Confidence: MEDIUM** -- ISPRA publication confirms the cedar/eucalyptus; waterfall/spring information from Wikipedia. No recent horticultural assessment found.

### 3.2 Geology of Laconi -- COMPLEX AND PROBLEMATIC

This is the most geologically complex of the three zones. The Sarcidano sits at the intersection of multiple geological units:

**Key finding from Sardegna Speleologica (1998)**: reference to "Tacco del Sarcidano (Laconi)" -- a "tacco" is a flat-topped limestone mesa, typical of the Sarcidano region. This indicates **calcareous (limestone) formations** present in the area.

**From Wikipedia/Laconi**: "Il suo vasto territorio che si estende per quasi 125 km2 e tra i piu vari e ricchi dal punto di vista naturalistico e **geologico** della Sardegna" -- emphasizing geological variety.

**From archaeological sources**: Menhirs at Laconi are made of both basalt AND granite, indicating both rock types are locally available.

**Synthesis**: Laconi sits on a **geological boundary zone**:
- **Paleozoic basement** (schists/granite) -- acidic soils
- **Mesozoic-Cenozoic limestone** ("tacchi" = mesas/plateaux) -- alkaline soils (pH 7.5-8.5)
- **Tertiary volcanic rocks** (basalt) -- neutral soils
- The Giara di Gesturi (basaltic plateau) is nearby but NOT the same formation as Laconi town

**CRITICAL PROBLEM**: The presence of limestone "tacchi" means parts of Laconi's territory have **alkaline calcareous soils completely unsuitable for Japanese gardens**. However, other parts on the crystalline basement would be acidic. The **specific location within Laconi territory** matters enormously.

The Parco Aymerich's success with cedars and eucalyptus (which tolerate slightly acidic to neutral soils) does NOT prove the soil is acidic enough for Japanese maples. Cedrus libani tolerates pH 5.0-8.0.

**Confidence: MEDIUM** -- geological complexity is confirmed, but exact soil pH at Parco Aymerich is unknown. The mix of limestone and crystalline rocks means extreme local variation.

### 3.3 Rainfall at Laconi

No specific precipitation data found in the Piano di Gestione tables for Laconi (which is in Oristano province, the table I accessed covered that province but Laconi was not in the visible portion).

**Estimate by analogy**: Laconi at 580m in the Sarcidano, a continental interior position without the orographic advantage of Montiferru:
- Estimated: **650-800 mm/year**
- Less than Montiferru (no direct Mistral interception)
- More than lowland Campidano (~450mm)
- Similar to Barbagia foothills at equivalent altitude

**Confidence: LOW** -- pure estimate, no direct data found.

### 3.4 Accessibility -- BEST of the three

- **Oristano**: ~40 km, approximately 40 min
- **Cagliari**: ~70 km, approximately 1h10
- **Nearest hospital**: Ospedale San Martino, Oristano (~40 min). Also hospital in Isili nearby.
- **Road**: SS128 connects to SS131 (main Sardinian highway)
- Laconi is relatively central -- the most accessible of all three zones

**Confidence: HIGH** -- confirmed by English Wikipedia ("70 km north of Cagliari, 40 km east of Oristano").

## STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

### MONTIFERRU (Santu Lussurgiu) = HIGHEST SCORE (47)

**Strengths**: Unmatched rainfall (960mm official), exceptional water springs, natural wind shelter in volcanic bowl, chestnuts and holly confirming adequate soil acidity, cultural sophistication (Gramsci studied here, Salesians, horse culture).

**Weaknesses**: Soil is marginally acidic (pH ~6.0-6.5) vs granite ideal (5.0-5.5). Would need some soil amendment (sulfur, peat, pine bark mulch) for the most acid-demanding species (Rhododendron, Pieris). **2021 wildfire devastation** is a serious concern -- many forests are recovering. Fire risk in summer is structurally high due to scirocco conditions.

**Best microsite**: Eastern slopes of the crater/bowl around the town itself, near one of the named springs, at 500-700m. Avoid the Abbasanta plateau (too alkaline, too exposed).

### GOCEANO (Bono/Burgos area) = SECOND (42)

**Strengths**: BEST soil chemistry by far -- granite+schist = pH 4.5-5.5, confirmed by chestnuts, yew, holly. Magnificent existing forests as backdrop. Tirso river + abundant mountain springs. Strong existing forestry infrastructure (Agenzia Forestas).

**Weaknesses**: Rainfall likely lower than Montiferru (estimated, not measured). Wind exposure on the ridge. More remote/isolated. The area is culturally depopulating.

**Best microsite**: Eastern or southern mid-slopes of Monte Rasu (700-900m), near the Monte Pisanu chestnut groves, sheltered from NW Mistral, near existing springs. The Sos Nibberos yew forest area (~1000m) would be too high and cold but demonstrates what the substrate can support.

### SARCIDANO / LACONI = THIRD (36, with caveats)

**Strengths**: Parco Aymerich is a PROVEN precedent of exotic arboriculture with natural spring irrigation. Best accessibility. Central position.

**Weaknesses**: **Geological uncertainty is the critical flaw.** The mix of limestone and crystalline rock means a garden could end up on pH 8 calcareous soil if the wrong parcel is chosen. This would be fatal for acid-loving Japanese garden plants. Lower rainfall. Less natural wind protection.

**Recommendation for Laconi**: A thorough **soil pH survey** of candidate parcels is essential BEFORE commitment. If a specific parcel near Parco Aymerich can be confirmed as being on crystalline (non-calcareous) substrate, it could work. But this requires actual soil testing, not just geological inference.
