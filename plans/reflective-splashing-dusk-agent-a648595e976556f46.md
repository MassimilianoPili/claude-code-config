## 2. Water in Goceano -- CRITICAL GAP

### What the sources confirm

**From Wikipedia Bono (T7):**
> "I versanti del monte Rasu sono solcati da una fitta rete di limpide sorgenti, ruscelli e torrenti a regime stagionale che scorrono all'interno dei boschi primigeni. Queste acque montane defluiscono verso sud, andando ad alimentare il bacino del Tirso, il fiume piu lungo della Sardegna, che lambisce la parte meridionale e piu mite del territorio comunale. La zona montana e inoltre caratterizzata e impreziosita dalla presenza di numerosi laghetti."

**Translation:** The slopes of Monte Rasu are crossed by a dense network of clear springs, streams and seasonal torrents flowing through the primeval forests. These mountain waters flow south, feeding the Tirso basin (Sardinia's longest river), which skirts the southern, milder part of the municipal territory. The mountain zone is also characterized by numerous small lakes.

**From Wikipedia Bono -- History (T7):**
The founders called the settlement "Bidda Sana" (Healthy Village) and then "Bono Loco" (Good Place) to celebrate "l'abbondanza di sorgenti pure" (the abundance of pure springs).

### Tirso River Headwaters

- The Tirso (152 km, longest river in Sardinia) has its headwaters in the Goceano chain
- From Wikipedia Tirso: it rises in the area between Budduso and the Goceano mountains
- The Tirso flows through/past the southern part of Bono's territory (the valley floor, ~225-300m elevation)
- **Water drawing rights:** In Italy, drawing water from public watercourses requires a "concessione di derivazione idrica" from the Regione. This is feasible but bureaucratic. For small agricultural use (< a certain threshold), the process is simpler.

### Wells in Schist/Granite Substrate

- Bono's geology: "scisti paleozoici e intrusioni granitiche" (Paleozoic schists and granitic intrusions) -- confirmed from Wikipedia
- **Drilling wells in schist/granite:** This is possible but challenging and expensive
  - Typical depth: 50-150m in crystalline rock, depending on fracture zones
  - Yield: Highly variable -- some boreholes hit fractures with good flow, others are dry
  - Cost: EUR 50-100/linear meter for drilling in hard rock in Sardinia
  - A hydrogeological survey is essential before drilling
  - Schist is generally more favorable than massive granite (more fracture planes)
  - **The abundant springs on Monte Rasu suggest good fractured aquifer potential in the area**

### Water Assessment Summary

**Positive signals:**
- Wikipedia explicitly mentions "fitta rete di limpide sorgenti" (dense network of clear springs)
- Historical name "Bidda Sana" celebrates pure springs
- Multiple laghetti in the mountain zone
- Tirso river accessible in the valley portion

**Critical gaps:**
- No measured flow rates for any specific springs
- No data on seasonal reliability (regime stagionale = seasonal regime -- some may dry in summer)
- No information on whether springs at 700-900m (chestnut zone) are perennial
- Well feasibility depends on local fracture patterns -- needs site-specific survey

**Recommendation:** Before purchasing land, commission a hydrogeological survey (indagine idrogeologica) from a local geologist. Cost: EUR 2,000-5,000. This will identify springs, assess seasonal flow, and evaluate well drilling prospects.

## 3. Goceano Forests -- Detailed Map

### Forest Complex Structure

From SardegnaForeste.it (official Forestas agency -- T1 equivalent):

**Foresta Demaniale Monte Pisanu** (territory of Bono)
- **Status:** State/regional demanial forest since 1886 (Legge n. 3713 del 4 Marzo 1886)
- **Management:** Agenzia Forestas (ex Ente Foreste della Sardegna)
- **Key features:**
  - Dominated by **Roverella (Quercus pubescens)** up to highest elevations (>1000m)
  - At highest elevations: cespuglioso (shrubby) oaks with small mountain meadows
  - Lower elevations: roverella mixed with **Sughera (cork oak)** and/or **Leccio (holm oak)**
  - **Sughera prevails in the 700-900m band**, often with roverella and/or leccio
  - Notable monumental cork oak at locality "Pedra Ruias"
  - **Castagno (chestnut):** Used in the earliest reforestation campaigns (early 1900s)
  - Later: experimental plantations of pino nero laricio, pino domestico, pino d'Aleppo, cipresso sempreverde, cedro dell'Atlante
  - **Sos Nibberos** (NW slope of Monte Rasu, ~1000m): Italy's largest Taxus baccata forest, declared natural monument 1994. Millennial yews up to 16m tall, diameter >1m. Rare endemic Rubus arrigonii in understory.
  - **Rinaturalizzazione underway:** Removing exotic conifers, restoring native oak woodland
  - East slope of Monte Rasu: **Roverella + Acero minore (Acer monspessulanum)** -- spectacular autumn colors

**Foresta Demaniale Fiorentini** (territory of Anela/Bultei)
- **Status:** Also established 1886 (same law) -- one of the oldest demanial forests in Sardinia
- **SIC (Sito di Importanza Comunitaria):** Listed among 114 Sardinian sites in Rete Natura 2000
- **Key localities:**
  - "Sa Fraigada" -- ancient church + splendid **bosco di castagni secolari** (centuries-old chestnut grove)
  - "Su Labiolaiu", "Su Tassu", "Sa Pruna" (Tomba dei Giganti archaeological site)
  - "Monte Unturzu" -- toponym recalls past presence of griffon vultures
  - "Madonna di Fatima" -- main service center with restored buildings, visitor facilities
- **Tourism infrastructure:** Good reception structures, rest areas, caserma forestale
- **Archaeology:** Tomba dei Giganti and nuraghi at Sa Pruna

**Foresta di Burgos** [COULD NOT VERIFY -- Wikipedia page does not exist at expected URL]
- Known to be adjacent to Burgos castle area
- Likely managed by Forestas

**Sos Nibberos** (within Monte Pisanu complex, Bono territory)
- NW slopes of Monte Rasu, ~1000m
- Monumento Naturale since 1994
- Italy's most extensive yew forest
- Millennial specimens
- Rich understory: agrifoglio (holly), ferns, endemic plants

### Ownership: State Forest vs Private

- **Demanial forests** (Monte Pisanu, Fiorentini, etc.) are public property managed by Forestas -- you cannot buy this land
- **However:** Private land exists between and adjacent to the demanial perimeters
- In the Goceano, the pattern is: valley floor = private agricultural, mid-slopes = mixed private/demanial, upper slopes = mostly demanial
- **Buying adjacent to state forest:** Legal and common. The constraints are:
  - **Vincolo idrogeologico:** Most mountain land in Sardinia has this constraint -- limits earthworks, requires permits for construction
  - **Vincolo paesaggistico:** Landscape protection -- any modification requires Soprintendenza approval
  - **SIC/Natura 2000:** If the land is within or adjacent to a SIC, environmental impact assessment may be needed
  - **Piano Paesaggistico Regionale (PPR):** Sardinia's landscape plan heavily restricts coastal development but is more permissive inland -- Goceano is not on the coast
  - **Uso civico:** Some land in Sardinia has "usi civici" (civic use rights) -- communal grazing/wood rights. Check with the Comune.

### Private Land with Chestnuts at 700-900m East Slope

- SardegnaForeste confirms chestnuts exist in the area (used in first reforestation campaigns, centuries-old grove at Sa Fraigada/Fiorentini)
- Cork oak prevails at 700-900m on Monte Pisanu
- **Private chestnut groves between 700-900m are plausible** but not specifically confirmed
- The east slope of Monte Rasu (roverella + acero minore zone) would be the zone to investigate
- Key question for a local geometra: "Ci sono terreni privati con castagni tra i 700 e i 900 metri sul versante est del Monte Rasu?"

## 5. Getting to Bono -- Transport Analysis

### Airport Distances (estimated from geography -- [NEEDS VERIFICATION with routing])

**From Olbia Costa Smeralda Airport (OLB):**
- Distance: ~95-110 km
- Route: SS131 DCN (Carlo Felice direction) or SS389 via Budduso
- Driving time: ~1h 30min - 1h 50min
- Road quality: Good highway-grade road (SS131 DCN) for most of the way, then provincial roads for the last 20-30 km

**From Alghero-Fertilia Airport (AHO):**
- Distance: ~110-130 km
- Route: SS291 to Sassari, then SS131 south, then inland via Ozieri
- Driving time: ~1h 40min - 2h
- Road quality: Good for the SS131 portion, narrower for the final stretch

**From Cagliari-Elmas Airport (CAG):**
- Distance: ~180-200 km
- Route: SS131 north through the entire island
- Driving time: ~2h 30min - 3h
- Road quality: SS131 is the main north-south artery, good dual carriageway for most of it

### Last 20 km to Bono

- Fully paved roads
- Winding mountain roads typical of Sardinia's interior (SP -- strade provinciali)
- Some stretches may lack guardrails
- In winter: occasional ice at the highest passes (>800m), very rare snow at town level (540m)
- No particular problems for a normal car -- a 4WD is useful only for accessing forest tracks

### Public Transport

- **Bus:** ARST (Azienda Regionale Sarda Trasporti) operates bus connections, but service is infrequent (1-2 buses/day to Ozieri/Sassari). Not practical for regular commuting.
- **Train:** The nearest train station is likely Ozieri-Chilivani (Trenitalia mainline). From there, bus or car to Bono (~30 km).
- **No direct train to Bono.**
- Practical reality: a car is essential.

### What can be inferred from geography and general Sardinian climatology

**Bono town altitude:** 540m -- territory ranges from 225m (Tirso valley) to 1,259m (Monte Rasu peak)

**Expected climate (Mediterranean montane):**
- **Classification:** Csb (Mediterranean with warm summer) to Csa at lower elevations
- **Precipitation:** 700-1000 mm/year at 500-800m (significantly more than coastal Sardinia's 400-600mm)
  - Mountain areas above 800m likely receive 900-1200 mm/year
  - Most rain: October-March; dry summer (June-August), but less severe than coastal areas
  - The dense forest cover on Monte Rasu is itself proof of adequate precipitation
- **Temperature:**
  - January average: 4-7C at 540m, -2 to 2C at 1000m+
  - July average: 22-25C at 540m, 17-20C at 1000m+
  - Summer maxima: rarely >35C at 540m (the altitude provides relief from Sardinia's heat)
- **Snow:**
  - At 540m (town): 2-5 days/year with snow, rarely accumulates significantly
  - At 800m: 5-15 snow days/year, occasional accumulation of 10-30cm
  - At 1000m+: 10-25 snow days/year, snow can persist for days
  - Wikipedia includes a photo "Neve Bono" -- snow does occur
- **Frost:** Regular in winter at all elevations; growing season ~March-November at 540m, shorter at higher elevations

**Key for Japanese garden:**
- The 700-900m zone would have adequate precipitation (800-1000 mm) without irrigation for many species
- Summer drought is the main challenge -- even at altitude, July-August can be dry
- The acid soil (pH 4.5-5.5 on schist/granite) is ideal for Japanese maples, azaleas, mosses, camellias
- Winter cold is moderate -- comparable to USDA zone 8b-9a, adequate for most Japanese garden plants
