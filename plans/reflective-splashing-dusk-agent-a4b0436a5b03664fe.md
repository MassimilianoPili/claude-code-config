## 1. RAINFALL DATA

### 1.1 General Pattern (MEASURED — ARPAS 1981-2010 + Ente Idrografico 1922-2014)

**Two seasons:** Wet (October-April), Dry (May-September). November is the wettest month; dry season can extend to November in southern areas or start as early as April.

**Driest stations (1922-1991 climatology):**
- Capo Carbonara: **381 mm/year** (absolute minimum)
- Capo Sperone (Sant'Antioco): **386 mm/year**
- Central Basso Campidano: slightly above **400 mm/year**
- Sestu: driest ARPAS station in 1981-2010 period

**Wettest stations (1922-1991 climatology):**
- **Vallicciola (Limbara, 1040m): 1343 mm/year** — wettest monitored station
- **Correboi (Gennargentu E slope): 1285 mm/year** (station now dismantled)
- **Sicca d'Erba (Gennargentu E slope): 1265 mm/year** (station now dismantled)

**ARPAS 1981-2010 updated climatology:**
- Only ONE station exceeds 1000 mm: **Desulo: 1054 mm/year**
- Many historical high-rainfall stations (Correboi, Pira e Onni, etc.) were dismantled >30 years ago
- This means the 1981-2010 data UNDERESTIMATES peak mountain rainfall

### 1.4 Zones Exceeding Thresholds

**>900 mm/year (MEASURED or highly probable):**
- Limbara massif (Vallicciola: 1343 mm)
- Gennargentu eastern slopes (Correboi: 1285, Sicca d'Erba: 1265)
- Desulo area (1054 mm ARPAS modern data)
- Fonni and surroundings (likely ~950-1050)

**>1000 mm/year:**
- Only Desulo exceeds 1000mm in the modern ARPAS network
- Historically: Vallicciola, Correboi, Sicca d'Erba all >1200mm
- Probable but not monitored: upper Gennargentu, upper Limbara, upper Montiferru

### 1.5 Wettest Zones Ranking

1. **Limbara (NE Sardinia, Gallura)** — Vallicciola at 1343mm is the all-time wettest monitored station. The Limbara massif (1362m peak) intercepts both NW (Maestrale) and NE moisture. This is the wettest measured zone in Sardinia.

2. **Gennargentu eastern slopes** — Correboi (1285mm), Sicca d'Erba (1265mm). East-facing slopes receive intense but less frequent rains from Grecale/Levante. Higher intensity per rain day.

3. **Montiferru (west-facing)** — Not well-monitored at altitude, but west-facing slopes intercept Maestrale/Ponente/Libeccio persistently. Likely 900-1100mm at 800-1000m. The western coast orientation gives more rainy DAYS (higher frequency, lower intensity per event).

4. **Goceano-Marghine** — Interior chain, moderately exposed. Likely 800-950mm at 600-800m.

### 1.6 Summer Rainfall (June-August)

**Critical for Japanese garden: this is the DRIEST period everywhere in Sardinia.**

From sardegna-clima.it: "Dal mese di Maggio la Sardegna comincia a entrare nella fascia di alte pressioni nord Africane... clima generalmente monotono e secco con frequenti giornate serene."

However, there are DIFFERENCES:

- **Eastern mountains (Gennargentu east face):** "In estate le correnti occidentali spingono l'aria calda sul sud est dell'isola dove occasionalmente si sviluppano intense celle temporalesche pomeridiane che interessano con maggiore frequenza i rilievi che guardano verso la costa orientale." — Some summer convective rainfall on east-facing mountain slopes.

- **Western slopes/interior:** Almost total summer drought. The prevailing Maestrale brings dry, stable air in summer.

- **Limbara/Gallura:** Occasional summer thunderstorms possible due to altitude effects, but NOT reliable. The area has less total summer drought than low-altitude Campidano but still very dry June-August.

**Bottom line:** NO zone in Sardinia has reliable summer rain. All garden sites will need irrigation June-September. Eastern mountain slopes get occasional summer storms; western slopes get almost nothing.

## 2. GEOLOGICAL SUBSTRATE & SOIL pH

### 2.1 Simplified Geological Map of Sardinia

Sardinia has one of the most complex geologies in Italy, with rocks spanning from Precambrian to Quaternary. Simplified:

**A. GRANITES (Hercynian basement, ~300 Ma) — ACIDIC pH 4.5-6.5**
- **Gallura (NE):** The dominant lithology. The entire Tempio Pausania-Aggius-Calangianus-Arzachena-Olbia area is granitic. The Limbara massif (1362m) is a massive monzogranite/leucogranite intrusion. (Source: ISPRA CARG Arzachena sheet — "monzograniti del massiccio di Tempio Pausania")
- **NE Sardinia generally:** Budduso, Ala dei Sardi, Bitti — granitic substrate
- **Southwestern mountains:** Iglesiente-Sulcis has granite + older metamorphic rocks
- **Central-eastern Barbagia:** Partially granitic (mixed with schists)

**B. METAMORPHIC SCHISTS (Paleozoic) — ACIDIC pH 4.5-6.0**
- **Central-eastern Sardinia:** Barbagia, Mandrolisai (Desulo, Aritzo, Tonara area)
- **Gennargentu:** Mixed granite and schist
- **Goceano (partly):** See below

**C. VOLCANIC ROCKS — Variable pH**
- **Montiferru:** Pliocene-Quaternary stratovolcano. Rocks: basalt, trachyte, phonolite, trachybasalt. pH depends on rock type:
  - **Basalt:** slightly acidic to neutral (pH 6.0-7.0)
  - **Trachyte:** neutral to slightly alkaline (pH 6.5-7.5)
  - Overall Montiferru soils: **neutral to slightly acidic (pH 6.0-7.0)**
- **Marghine chain (Bolotana area):** Mixed. The SIA document states: "a Burgos affiorano basalti da alcalini ad..." — basaltic outcrops in the Burgos area. The Marghine chain has Cenozoic basaltic/volcanic covers over a Paleozoic basement. Bolotana sits at the northern foot of the Marghine where volcanic cover meets the Goceano basement.
- **Logudoro (Campeda, Abbasanta plateaux):** Basaltic plateaux (giare). Neutral to slightly acidic soils.

**D. LIMESTONE/DOLOMITE — ALKALINE pH 7.0-8.5**
- **Supramonte:** Oliena, Orgosolo, Urzulei, Dorgali — massive Mesozoic limestone (Jurassic-Cretaceous)
- **Eastern coast (Golfo di Orosei):** Limestone cliffs
- **Iglesiente:** Paleozoic limestone
- **Nurra (NW):** Mesozoic limestone
- **Tacchi (Sarcidano):** Isolated limestone plateaux near Laconi, Seui, Esterzili

### 2.2 Goceano (Bono, Burgos) — ANSWER

The Goceano is geologically MIXED:
- **Basement:** Paleozoic metamorphic rocks (schists, phyllites) — ACIDIC
- **Volcanic cover:** Cenozoic basaltic flows, especially around Burgos: "a Burgos affiorano basalti da alcalini ad..." (SIA document)
- **Granite:** Present in some areas (the SIA mentions "graniti della Sardegna settentrionale" in context of Goceano building materials)

**Bono:** Sits on the Goceano basement — primarily metamorphic schists + some granite. **Substrate: ACIDIC (pH ~5.0-6.5)**

**Burgos:** Has basaltic outcrops overlying the metamorphic basement. **Substrate: MIXED — areas with basalt (pH 6.0-7.0) and areas with schist (pH 5.0-6.0)**

### 2.3 Marghine (Bolotana) — ANSWER

The Marghine chain is a NW-SE oriented ridge between Goceano and Campeda. Bolotana sits on its northern slope.
- The chain is topped by **Cenozoic basaltic/volcanic rocks** (a continuation of the same volcanism that created the Campeda plateau)
- Below the volcanic cap: **Paleozoic metamorphic basement**
- **Bolotana substrate: predominantly BASALTIC/volcanic cover at higher elevations, metamorphic below. pH: 6.0-7.0 (volcanic) to 5.0-6.0 (metamorphic)**

### 2.4 Montiferru Volcanic Rocks — ANSWER

The Montiferru (Monte Urtigu, 1050m) is a Plio-Pleistocene stratovolcano with diverse products:
- **Trachyte and trachybasalt** (main edifice) — pH 6.5-7.5
- **Basalt** (lava flows, especially lower slopes) — pH 6.0-7.0
- **Phonolite** (minor) — pH 6.5-7.0

The soils derived from these volcanic rocks are generally **andosols or cambisols** with moderate to high fertility, **pH typically 6.0-7.0** (neutral to slightly acidic). This is LESS acidic than granite or schist, but NOT alkaline like limestone.

**For a Japanese garden: Montiferru volcanic soils are workable. Not naturally acidic enough for extreme acid-lovers (like some azaleas that want pH 4.5-5.5), but amenable to amendment. Much better than limestone.**

### 2.5 Cork Oak (Quercus suber) Distribution — PROXY FOR ACIDIC SOIL

Cork oak is a perfect acidophile indicator (requires pH 4.5-6.5, siliceous substrates). From ISPRA and Piano Forestale:

**Major cork oak forests in Sardinia:**
1. **Gallura** — THE heartland of Sardinian cork. Calangianus is the "cork capital" of Italy. Dense sugherete around Tempio Pausania, Calangianus, Luras, Aggius, Berchidda.
2. **Monte Limbara slopes** — extensive sugherete on granitic substrate
3. **Budduso-Ala dei Sardi** — large sugherete on granite
4. **Bitti-Lodine-Gavoi area** — on schist/granite
5. **Nulvi-Sedini (Anglona)** — on volcanic/granite
6. **Iglesiente-Sulcis** — scattered sugherete on granitic/metamorphic substrate
7. **Monte Linas-Gutturu Mannu** — near Villacidro, on granite/schist

**Cork oak is ABSENT or rare on:**
- Limestone (Supramonte, Nurra)
- Campidano alluvial plains
- Highest elevations (>800m generally too cold)

**The Gallura-Tempio-Calangianus cork belt is the STRONGEST confirmation of deep acidic soils in that area.**

### 2.6 Chestnut (Castanea sativa) Distribution — ACIDOPHILE INDICATOR

Chestnut requires acidic, deep, well-drained soils (pH 5.0-6.5). From ISPRA and Piano Forestale:

**Main chestnut areas in Sardinia (very limited):**
- **Aritzo-Belvi** — the main concentration (Baragliu 1978, Piano Forestale)
- **Desulo** — some presence
- **Tonara** — minor
- **Laconi** — ISPRA notes chestnut "limitati all'area di Belvi, Aritzo, Laconi e Tonara"
- **Sorgono** — margin
- **Bottida** — a monumental chestnut ("Pedra Ruja" — Piano Paesaggistico)

All these are on **schist or granite substrate**. The presence of chestnut at Laconi and Aritzo-Belvi confirms acidic soils in those specific areas (despite Laconi being near the limestone Tacchi — the chestnut grows on the NON-limestone portions).

## 3. TEMPERATURE DATA

### 3.1 General Pattern (sardegna-clima.it)

**Coastal:** Very mild winters. Cagliari has among Italy's warmest winter means. Mean winter temp ~10C at coast.

**Hills (300-500m):** Winter mean 5-7C.

**~1000m altitude:** Winter mean ~3-4C.

**>1500-1600m:** Mean winter temp goes NEGATIVE.

**Summer:** Mean >26C in southern coast, Tirso valley, Flumendosa valley. Western coast ~24.5C (cooler, upwind). Below 20C mean only above ~1000m.

### 4.2 Gallura (Tempio area) — Water Rich or Poor? ANSWER

**The Gallura is RELATIVELY water-rich** for Sardinia, for several reasons:

1. **Rainfall:** Tempio gets ~785mm/year, and the nearby Limbara massif gets >1300mm — feeding streams and springs.
2. **Lago del Liscia (105 Mm3):** Major reservoir in the Gallura, fed by the Liscia river which drains the Limbara and surrounding granite hills. This reservoir serves much of Gallura's water supply.
3. **Springs:** The SIA environmental study mentions a mineral water concession "Monte Limpas" between Bortigiadas and Tempio, indicating the presence of quality groundwater. Granite typically has **limited but reliable springs** where fractures and weathering create local aquifers.
4. **Granite hydrology caveat:** Granitic rock has LOW primary porosity — groundwater moves through fractures, not through the rock matrix. This means:
   - Springs are small but numerous (from fracture intersections)
   - No large karstic aquifers (unlike limestone areas)
   - Streams run even in dry periods thanks to slow fracture drainage
   - Wells can be productive if they hit fracture zones

**Comparison with other areas:**
- **Limestone areas (Supramonte)** have more groundwater storage but it's deep and hard to access
- **Campidano plains** have alluvial aquifers but severe over-exploitation
- **Gallura granite** provides moderate, reliable water — ADEQUATE for a garden with supplementary irrigation from Liscia system

### 4.3 Areas Known for Springs

- **Limbara massif:** Numerous springs on all slopes, feeding Tempio's historical fountains (Funtana Pastini, etc. — "Architetture dell'Acqua in Sardegna")
- **Goceano (Bono/Burgos):** Sos Canales reservoir; springs from metamorphic/basalt contact zones
- **Gennargentu:** Numerous springs, but remote
- **Montiferru (Santu Lussurgiu):** Volcanic permeability creates springs at basalt/impermeable contact zones. The San Leonardo spring system is famous.

## 5. SYNTHESIS — BEST LOCATIONS FOR A JAPANESE GARDEN

### Ranking by Combined Criteria

| Location | Rainfall | Soil pH | Winter Temp | Water | Overall |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tempio Pausania / Aggius** | 785mm (good) | GRANITE: 5.0-6.0 (excellent for acid-lovers) | Moderate frost, rarely <-7C | Good (Liscia + springs) | **BEST** |
| **Santu Lussurgiu** | ~850-900mm (very good) | VOLCANIC: 6.0-7.0 (workable, not ideal) | Light frost | Good (springs) | **VERY GOOD** |
| **Bono / Burgos** | ~800mm (good) | MIXED schist/basalt: 5.0-7.0 | Moderate frost | Moderate (Sos Canales) | **GOOD** |
| **Bolotana** | ~750mm (adequate) | BASALT/schist: 5.5-7.0 | Moderate frost | Moderate | **GOOD** |
| **Laconi** | ~750mm (adequate) | MIXED (schist ACIDIC areas + limestone ALKALINE nearby) | Light frost | Moderate | **GOOD but verify specific plot** |
| **Desulo** | 1054mm (excellent) | SCHIST: 5.0-6.0 (excellent) | Occasional severe frost -9C | Remote, limited | **GOOD for moisture, risky for cold** |
| **Villacidro** | ~650mm (marginal) | GRANITE/schist: 5.0-6.0 (good) | Very mild | Moderate | **TOO DRY, too low** |
| **Fonni** | ~1000mm (excellent) | GRANITE/schist: good | TOO COLD (-17C at nearby stations) | Good | **TOO COLD** |

### Why Tempio Pausania / Aggius is the top candidate:

1. **The granite substrate produces exactly the acidic soil (pH 5.0-6.0) that Japanese maples, azaleas, camellias, and moss require.** The presence of dense cork oak forests and (historically) some chestnut confirms this.

2. **Rainfall of ~785mm plus proximity to Limbara (1343mm)** means the landscape is greener than most of Sardinia. The Limbara springs provide additional water.

3. **The Lago del Liscia (105 Mm3)** provides a reliable water supply for irrigation during the dry summer months.

4. **Winter temperatures are moderate** — cold enough for the seasonal dormancy that temperate Japanese plants need, with occasional snow (aesthetically perfect for a Japanese garden), but NOT cold enough to kill semi-hardy species.

5. **The granite landscape itself is quintessentially "Japanese"** — rounded boulders (tafoni), weathered rock formations, existing moss colonies. The stazzi landscape of Gallura already has an aesthetic kinship with Japanese garden aesthetics.

6. **Existing vegetation analogy:** Cork oak + strawberry tree (Arbutus unedo) + heather (Erica arborea) landscape is structurally similar to a Japanese satoyama woodland. The transition to Japanese garden design would be natural.

### Specific recommendation within the Tempio-Aggius area:

Look for a site at **500-700m elevation**, on the **northern or eastern slope of Limbara**, close to natural springs, with existing granite boulders and mature cork oaks. The village of **Aggius** (514m, on granite, near Limbara, known for its boulder landscape "Valle della Luna") is perhaps the most aesthetically promising micro-location.
